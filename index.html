Turtles

Check out these facts about turtles

Some Fast Facts

  • Turtles are reptiles.
  • Turtles have a hard shell that protects them like a shield, this upper shell is called a ‘carapace’.
  • Turtles also have a lower shell called a ‘plastron’.
  • Many turtle species (not all) can hide their heads inside their shells when attacked by predators.
  • Turtles have existed for around 215 million years.
  • Like other reptiles, turtles are cold blooded.
  • The largest turtle is the leatherback sea turtle, it can weigh over 900 kg! (2000 lb) Turtles lay eggs.

How To Protect Turtles

  1. Turn Out Lights Visible From the Beach!
  2. Reduce the Amount of Garbage You Produce and Clean Up Trash You See On the Beach.
  3. Be Aware of Sea Turtle Nesting Areas and Avoid Nesting an Hatching Turtles.
  4. Reduce the Amount of Chemicals You Use.
